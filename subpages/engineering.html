Technical Events

Structural Designer (Civil)
Rules

Structural engineers are primarily concerned with analyzing and designing various types of structures. This is an event where the participants showcase their knowledge of IS Codes and design skills using structural design software such as ETABS and STAAD PRO. Come, participate and test your limit state!

Innovative CAD Designer (Civil)
Rules

In this competition, participants have to design a double-line architectural plan, front elevation and sectional elevation passing through the given information from a single-line diagram using AutoCAD. This event will surely help you bring out creativity into an architectural drawing showing your innovative abilities as a civil engineer.

Spark-a-thon
Rules

Particpants are expected to come out with fresh and innovative solutions to existing challenges in the society. The ideas should be novel and helpful for the common public. Participants usually work in teams, present multiple ideas and solutions, subsequently working toward the most suitable of the lot.

C-Talash
Rules

C-Talash, a team-based orientation activity for incoming freshmen in the Department of Computer Science. It is predominantly aimed to teach programming concepts through interactive games with interesting narratives. Games have been developed to teach basic concepts of programming such as deriving logic, writing code, debugging the code and so on.

Aphid (ECE)
Rules

Aphid is a technical event conducted by the department of the ECE. Aphid like cicada is an Hemiptera. Here aphid is a smaller version of the infamous cicada 3301 where participants can test their skills and ingenuity on a smaller scale in order to crack the event and win prizes. This event is perfect for anyone who likes mysteries or thinking outside the box.

Fidelity (ECE)
Rules

Fidelity is an event conducted by department of Electronics and Communication. Fidelity is an event aimed to test the technical skills of the participants and their ability to use the given tools (like Matlab) to solve a number of increasingly difficult questions. Fidelity aims to make this experience relaxed and enjoyable for the participants ensuring they have some key take aways.

Watt's the Buzz? (EEE)
Rules

The department of EEE presents 'Watt's the Buzz?', a multi-level quiz event that aims at developing competency in research & product knowledge with interdisciplinary thinking in avenues that include improved analytics and better knowledge about current developing topics in the field of Engineering & Technology. Participants will have 3 interactive live rounds with quiz Masters.

Ohm-azing Build-a-thon (EEE)
Rules

The department of EEE presents 'Ohm-azing Build-a-thon', a multi-level time-based event that aims to enhance the knowledge in research and development. It will provide participants with the opportunity to learn about the basics of electrical circuits and how they work, as well as hands-on experience building circuits. This will be a time-based event with 5 rounds in succession.
