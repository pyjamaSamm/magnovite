Engineering Events

Structural Designer (Civil)

Structural engineers are primarily concerned with analyzing and designing various types of structures. This is an event where the participants showcase their knowledge of IS Codes and design skills using structural design software such as ETABS and STAAD PRO. Come, participate and test your LIMIT STATE!!

Innovative CAD designer (Civil)

AutoCAD is a computer-aided design software developed by Autodesk. It allows you to draw and edit digital 2D and 3D designs more quickly and easily than you could by hand. In this competition, students have to design a double-line architectural plan, front elevation and sectional elevation passing through the given information from a single-line diagram.
This event will surely help you bring out creativity into an architectural drawing showing your innovative abilities as a civil engineer.

Spark-a-thon

Spark-a-thon is a technical brainstorming event where students are expected to come out with fresh and innovative solutions to existing challenges in the society. The ideas should be novel and helpful for the common public. Participants usually work in teams, present multiple ideas and solutions, subsequently working toward the most suitable of the lot.

C-Talash

C-Talash, a team-based orientation activity for incoming freshmen in the Department of Computer Science.
C-Talash is predominantly aimed to teach programming concepts through interactive games with interesting narratives. Games have been developed to teach basic concepts of programming such as deriving logic, writing code, debugging the code and so on.

Aphid (ECE)

Aphid is a technical event conducted by the department of the ECE for anyone eager enough to participate. Aphid like cicada is an Hemiptera. Cicada 3301 was the nickname given to three sets of puzzles posted under the name "3301" online between 2012 and 2014.
Here aphid is a smaller version of the infamous cicada 3301 where participants can test their skills and ingenuity on a smaller scale in order to crack the event and win prizes. This event is perfect for anyone who likes mysteries or thinking outside the box.

Fidelity (ECE)

Fidelity is an event conducted by department of Electronics and Communication. Matlab is a multi-paradigm programming language and a numeric computing environment developed by mathworks.
Fidelity is an event aimed to test the technical skills of the participants and their ability to use the given tools to solve a number of increasingly difficult questions. Fidelity aims to make this experience relaxed and enjoyable for the participants ensuring they have some key take aways.

Watt’s the Buzz? (EEE)

Relevant knowledge in emerging technologies and engineering practices are the backbone of any engineer in any field or range. The department of EEE presents ‘Watt’s the Buzz?’, a multi-level quiz event that aims at developing competency in Research & Product Knowledge with Interdisciplinary thinking in avenues that include improved Analytics, better problem solving, and better knowledge about current developing topics in the field of Engineering & Technology.
Here the participants will have 3 Interactive live rounds with Quiz Masters. To make it more interesting, the first round will have no eliminations

Ohm-azing Build-a-thon (EEE)

The department of EEE presents ‘Ohm-azing Build-a-thon’, a multi-level time-based event that aims to enhance the knowledge in research and development. This event will provide participants with the opportunity to learn about the basics of electrical circuits and how they work, as well as hands-on experience building circuits.
It will be a fun and engaging way for participants to learn about electrical circuits, and to develop their problem-solving, critical thinking, and teamwork skills. This will be a time-based event with 5 rounds in succession. The team that completes all 5 rounds in the shortest period of time will be crowned the winner.
